**Case Study 1: Supermarket Inventory System**

A supermarket stores the prices of 10 items in an array.

Questions:

* Find the most expensive and cheapest item.
* Calculate the average price of all items.
* Count how many items cost more than ₹500.
* Sort the prices in ascending order and display them.





**Case Study 2: Bank Transaction History**

A bank account has 12 months of transaction amounts (positive = credit, negative = debit).

Questions:

* Calculate the net balance at end of year.
* Find the month with highest credit and highest debit.
* Count the number of credit and debit transactions separately.
* Display all months where the transaction was a debit.





**Case Study 3: E-Commerce Sales Data**

An online store tracks daily sales (in ₹) for a month (30 days).

Questions:

* Find the best selling day and worst selling day.
* Calculate total monthly revenue.
* Find days where sales exceeded ₹10,000.
* Implement a search to check if a specific sale amount exists in the data.





**Case Study 4: Airline Seat Booking System**

An airline has 50 seats. 1 = booked, 0 = available.

Questions:

* Count total booked and available seats.
* Display all available seat numbers.
* Book the first available seat and update the array.
* Check if a specific seat number is available or not.
* Find the last available seat in the flight.



**Case Study 5: Shopping Cart — Discount System**

A cart has 8 item prices stored in an array.

Questions:

* Apply a 10% discount on all items above ₹1000 and update prices.
* Find the most and least expensive item.
* Calculate total bill before and after discount.
* Remove an item by setting its price to 0, then display updated cart.
* Count items that are eligible for GST (price > ₹500).
